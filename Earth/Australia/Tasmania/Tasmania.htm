TASMANIA
 
Version 1.0

For 'The Second Century' edition of Railroad Tycoon II
 
 
Author : Cam Hills 


Requirement : The Second Century or newer.


Overview : Tasmania - Australia's 'Apple Isle' is set to become a major supplier of food to the mainland's cities.


Map Size : 400 x 400.


Duration : January 1895 to December 1920.


Map Base : Newly created map.


Restrictions :
  • No resigning as Chair.
  • Track must connect to own company's.
  • Limited list of managers.
  • Steam engines only.


Objectives :

  • Single Player Bronze:
    • 80 loads of produce to Launceston.
    • $25 million company book value.
  • Single Player Silver:
    • Bronze conditions.
    • $10 million personal net worth.
  • Single Player Gold:
    • Silver conditions.
    • 30 loads of food to Launceston.
    • $40 million company book value.
  • Multi Player Gold:
    • Highest company book value amongst human players.
    • Minimum company book value of $5 million.

Comments :

History

Rail in Tasmania was somewhat confined to the northern townships for much of the latter part of the Nineteenth Century. Although the first lines were established over a decade earlier than this game's commencement, these early lines were generally very short and did not encompass the expansive network that the successful player must construct in this scenario. The names of the 'robbers' used in the scenario were largely sourced from Australian bushrangers in Tasmania whose time of notoriety was also some years earlier than this scenario's starting date of 1895 (apologies to the purists!). While fresh produce was a major business in Tasmania at the time, it was in fact wool that was the principal industry - as such, wool is included as an export commodity at ports.

Game Objectives

The Single Player objectives require that the player does indeed create a company of at least a moderate size in order to achieve the Bronze condition. 

The Silver condition ensures that the player involve themselves with some stock market activity. 

The Gold condition requires a network that encompasses three distant areas of the map - in the vicinities of the central-north, the south-east, and the west - in order to run steel and produce to a cannery and then on to export via Launceston. 

The events structure has aimed to prevent some of the reward reaped from the 'stop trains' (or 'slow trains') tactic to achieve the personal net worth objective. At the advice of Steve Martineau, restrictions forbidding resigning as Company Chair was brought in to prevent the player from plundering AI companies.

The Multi Player objective of highest Company Book Value pertains only to other human companies, although there is a $5 million minimum to ensure that some form of competitive rail network is indeed established.
